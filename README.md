## My Game Takeaway

The NCL Spring 2026 season helped me sharpen my technical skills and think through problems under pressure.

The categories that connected most with my day-to-day security work was Log Analysis. Those challenges reinforced a lot of the same habits used in real security operations, like understanding the context, finding the important artifacts, filtering out noise, reading carefully, and most importantly, documenting what you find. It really felt like finding the needle in the haystack.

Even though I am mainly focused on the defensive side of cybersecurity, the offensive security categories were also really useful because they helped me build and better understand those skills. I think a security analyst who understands the attacker’s point of view has a much stronger approach to defense because they can better recognize how attacks actually happen. Working through web exploitation, enumeration, cryptography, and password cracking challenges gave me a better view of where detections can be improved and how small bugs can turn into real security risks.

Overall, The NCL games helped me confirm some of my current strengths, see where I still need to improve, and connect what I am learning in class with hands-on security work.
